palao

Afghani Palao, a fragrant rice dish from Afghanistan, blends basmati rice, tender meat, and aromatic spices like cumin and cardamom. Simmered to perfection, it embodies the sweet and savory harmony of Afghan cuisine, showcasing a delightful mix of flavors and cultural richness.

Here's a simple recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 lb lamb or chicken, cut into chunks
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup almonds of slivered almonds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • salt,to taste
  • pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups chicken or lamb broth
  • 1 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until they become golden brown.
  3. Add the chunks of lamb or chicken to the pot and brown them on all sides.
  4. Add cumin seeds,cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and bay leaf to the pot. Stir well to combine.
  5. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Stir the rice with the other ingredients to coat the grains with the spices and oil.
  6. Add the julienned carrots, raisins, and almonds to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Pour in the chicken or lamb broth and water. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  8. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  9. Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
  10. Serve the Afghani Palao hot, garnished with additional almonds and raisins if desired.

    11. Enjoy your delicious Afghani Palao
